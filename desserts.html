Sweet Spots in the Holy City

Downtown Charleston
Desserts & Bakeries

From French pastries on King Street to house-made cinnamon rolls — the best places to satisfy a sweet tooth downtown.

Charleston has always had a serious sweet tooth. The city's dessert scene runs the full range — from European-trained chocolatiers tucking hand-painted truffles behind Society Street storefronts, to neighborhood bakeries cranking out sticky buns and cupcakes that draw lines down the block on weekends.

"The best bakeries in Charleston feel less like storefronts and more like reasons to slow down."

What follows is our running list of downtown dessert shops worth knowing about. We keep it focused on the lower peninsula — walkable, honest, no sponsored spots.

The List

Dessert Shops & Bakeries Downtown

Christophe Artisan Chocolatier-Pâtissier
90 Society St — Between Meeting & King

One of the most distinctive dessert destinations in all of Charleston. Christophe is known for hand-painted chocolate truffles that are almost too beautiful to eat — almost. The almond croissants are flaky and buttery, and the overall pastry selection carries a genuine European pedigree. If you're after something truly artisan, this is the stop.

Chocolates Almond Croissants French Pastries Truffles
Carmella's Cafe & Dessert Bar
198 E Bay St — Downtown

A go-to for post-dinner dessert on East Bay. Carmella's covers the full spectrum — rich cakes, cheesecakes, baklava, and a rotating selection that skews indulgent in the best way. The atmosphere is warm and vintage-feeling, making it an easy spot to linger over something sweet after a long evening of exploring the city.

Cakes Cheesecake Baklava Dessert Bar
La Pâtisserie
404 King St — Inside Hotel Bennett

Tucked inside Hotel Bennett on lower King Street, La Pâtisserie brings genuine European pastry craft to one of the city's most prominent addresses. The tarts and eclairs are little works of art, and their pistachio croissant has quietly become one of the most talked-about pastries on King Street. Worth stopping in even if you're not a hotel guest.

Eclairs Tarts Pistachio Croissant Hotel Bennett
Kaminsky's Dessert Cafe
78 N Market St — City Market Area

A longtime Charleston institution steps from the City Market. Kaminsky's keeps a full dessert menu running alongside specialty coffees, cocktails, and milkshakes in a cozy, dimly lit space that draws both locals and visitors late into the evening. The rotating cake selection changes regularly — if something on the chalkboard catches your eye, order it.

Dessert Cafe Specialty Coffee Cocktails Late Night
Sugar Bakeshop
59½ Cannon St — Cannonborough

A small-batch neighborhood bakeshop with a devoted following for its cupcakes, macarons, and signature brownies. The chocolate, Nutella, and salted caramel fudge brownies in particular have a near-legendary reputation among locals. The vintage interior and community feel make it one of the most charming spots on this entire list.

Cupcakes Macarons Fudge Brownies Small Batch
Bakehouse Bakery Cafe
160 E Bay St — French Quarter

The French Quarter's resident bakery and coffee house, conveniently situated on East Bay between The Battery and the City Market. Bakehouse serves cakes, cookies, brownie bars, tarts, and quiche alongside their custom-roasted house blend coffee. It's the kind of spot that rewards a slow morning — grab a tart and a latte and find a table.

Tarts Brownie Bars House Roast Coffee French Quarter
Brown's Court Bakery
St. Philip St — Off King

A quietly beloved spot one block off King Street that the crowds haven't fully discovered yet. Fresh-baked pastries, bread, and breakfast items are crafted daily, and the whole operation has the feel of a neighborhood bakery that just happens to be very, very good. Worth the slight detour off the main strip.

Fresh Pastries Artisan Bread Hidden Gem Off King
Harken Cafe & Bakery
62 Queen St — French Quarter

While Harken earns its coffee shop stripes during morning hours, the bakery side deserves its own mention. Their seasonal pastries, quiche, and fresh cookies are made in-house daily. The chocolate chip cookie alone is worth stopping in for. Breakfast burritos with chorizo and avocado round out a menu that does both savory and sweet well.

Seasonal Pastries Cookies Quiche Scratch Kitchen
Sweet Palm Coffee & Food
471 King St — Downtown Charleston

Best known as one of King Street's top coffee spots, Sweet Palm also deserves a place on this list for one specific reason: their house-made cinnamon rolls. These aren't an afterthought — they're baked fresh in-house and have built a quiet but dedicated following among locals who know to show up early before they sell out. Pair one with a specialty latte or cold brew and you've got one of the more satisfying morning combinations downtown has to offer. The full breakfast and lunch menu makes it easy to turn a pastry run into a proper sit-down.

Cinnamon Rolls House-Made Pastries Specialty Coffee King Street Breakfast & Lunch
